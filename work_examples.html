Example 1

The Autuer Theory

You can read the full paper by downloading it on Microsoft Word or PDF format.

"As the screenwriter and co-director as well as playing the main role in the film, I believe it's enough to consider him [Ricky Gervais] an auteur at least for this film. Overall, taking into account Gervais’ style of films as part of the comedy genre, I would also qualify him as an auteur and The Invention of Lying as an auteurist film."

This paper was for a course I took called "Movie Criticisms" where at the time, we were primarily going over the auteur theory. In this paper, I use the film "The Invention of Lying as an example to not only discuss the auteur theory but also the ideas of idealism and class differences. An auteur is a filmmaker whose influence and artistic control is so high, that they are regarded as the author of the film. The Invention of Lying is a fun comedy by Ricky Gervais that takes on some deep concepts such as death and religion, however, I used all the roles that Gervais plays in the creation of the film to demonstrate how I consider him to be an auteur director.
